ive been into ttrpgs ever since my dad got me and my cousin to play dnd one boring week over at my grandmas. this was probably 2014.

since then ive played 4-5 campaigns and even ran my own which lasted about a year .. its really fun! i love dming and seeing my friends laugh and do bullshit and care about the lore
or not care and just exist in the world to dismember and torture sewer cultists. there arent many other places in life where you collaborate creatively i wonder why.

i keep coming back to this idea of osr games specifically the into the odd series of games. the mechanics are so elegant and there is something special about emphasizing creativity
in a less restrictive system than trying to assign stats and rules to everything. we have video games now this is not that. when i was a player of dnd my favorite part was looking 
through new modules and class lists and making character after character excited about what different powers i could have or ways i could play the game. actually playing dnd was often
a slog, repeating the one cool thing my build optimized for and shoving the bard up front every time there was a social situation.

im drawn to the idea of everyone being able to fill any role they want at any time, but also character development needs to be able to happen. this is a balance that is ultimately up 
to the dm i guess. players also need solid things in the world they can point to and work off of. a game with zero direction is not a successful one either. maybe an 80:20 dm/player
creative control balance is good?

i guess part of progression is being able to point to somewhere written down and say im good at a thing. and many of the "creative" solutions people come up with become boring the
second or third time theyre reused. either the dm should continually face the players with novel situations (which is extremely hard), or powers should come with limitations. a player
should never say "i'm just going to do the thing again" and go through the motions, they should have a goal, a way of getting there and a slight unique barrier they have to come up
with an idea to solve. this barrier also cant be so big as to make only the wildest solutions work, the game must be realistic (e.g. your leg is amputated > lets go look for a
prosthetics shop > \*rolls\* oh theres one in town > i pay money > great now youre the same as before) <- this is uninteresting!

i guess this is why i want to add more the mythic bastionland rules to ItO combat, people love combat, the added dice and tactics make it interesting! I don't know if people will
find a way to abuse the same bonuses every time though -> maybe thats a signal that the dm needs to react and have the enemies come up with a new way around that... thoughts.
